1 what it is

a voice-only concierge that lives on the hotel's website. a guest taps a mic and just talks — in German or English — and it answers like a receptionist: shows matching rooms as photo cards, checks live availability and prices, hands off a secure payment link, and when it can't help, points the guest straight to the team (phone + email on screen). it never makes anything up — every room, price and date comes from a live lookup in that same conversation.

  • voice only — the mic is the only input; the panel just shows things (photo cards, captions, tappable contact buttons). no typing anywhere.
  • German + English, automatic — it detects the guest's language each time and replies in it; no menu to pick.
  • grounded — a wrong price is a real legal/business problem, so nothing about rooms/prices is ever invented.

3 the voice (the 2026-07-07 pivot — SHIPPED to the widget)

call mode (primary): OpenAI gpt-realtime-2.1 speech-to-speech, voice marin — one model hears, thinks and speaks; laughter, tone progression and mid-sentence DE/EN switching are built into the model (the client's requirement: expressiveness can't be scripted onto a flat TTS). fallback (HTTP loop): Voxtral STT → Mistral → OpenAI TTS (gpt-4o-mini-tts, voice coral — marin/cedar are Realtime-API-exclusive). ElevenLabs "Linda" + LiveKit retired 2026-07-07 (client: "remove linda and fully wire in gpt" after A/B listening: "much more human than eleven-labs"). no voice cloning.
  • why the pivot: chained TTS performs a script; a speech-to-speech model carries its own emotional state across the call — the client's "Maya/Ex-Machina" bar (disclosed AI that still feels human).
  • persona: server/realtime-instructions.js, OpenAI's official 8-section structure (Role / Personality & Tone / Tools / Values / Rules / Unclear Audio / Flow / Language / Escalation) — includes the audited improvements: flexible-date scanning (up to 3 windows), "not bookable online" (never "fully booked"), spoken-form values (never "226.00 EUR" aloud), tool preambles.
  • speech & delivery layer (2026-07-07 — the client's "Maya from Sesame" ask): built from a research sweep of how others humanize gpt-realtime (OpenAI's realtime prompting guide + voice-agent metaprompt, LiveKit's "sound more realistic" guide). the levers: bilingual flat→alive example pairs (adjectives alone don't change delivery), filler + pause + connector timing ("um … so" — an "um" at full speed sounds fake), light laughter on happy news only (calm-warm baseline, big emotions sparingly, ONE mood per reply), one gentle self-correction max per call, one-or-two touches per reply, none is fine. clean-speech rule stated twice on purpose (research: redundancy makes rules stick): never a filler / laugh / pause / stutter inside a price, date, time, number or name.
    • mistake hit + fixed during testing: English-only example pairs pulled the model into English with a German guest (first de-4 sample) — examples are now bilingual and "examples show delivery, NOT language" is an explicit rule; the retest answered in German with clean numbers.
  • fallback TTS carries the same delivery steer as the call persona (the cached greeting WAV + its audio cache were retired with the on-open greeting 2026-07-07 — the only greeting is now spoken live in-call).
  • local context injection (built 2026-07-07, client's "fine rainy day" ask): server/local-context.js — local time (server clock, Europe/Berlin, free) + current Konz weather (BrightSky/DWD open data, keyless, 10-min cache, fails soft to time-only) appended to the instructions at session mint (zero in-call latency) and to the fallback system prompt; steers greetings/small talk/suggestions, marked "CURRENT moment only, not a forecast" and "NOT a language cue". first live test: "Since it's a warm afternoon, how about starting with something outdoors?".
  • language + narration hardening (built 2026-07-07 after A/B sample runs): language rule pinned at the TOP of the prompt (every reply 100% in the guest's language), switch only on a SUBSTANTIVE utterance (never on borrowed words like "das Frühstück"), internal-reasoning narration banned aloud, honesty rule (never claims human). reasoning {effort: low} was probe-accepted, A/B-tested and REJECTED — it collapsed DE/EN discipline (two full English replies to a German guest); production keeps the default. known rig flake: the late-arrival worry turn sometimes opens with ONE English line in the tool-less rig; the live-call pass is the true check. samples: test_users/gpt-realtime/out-continue/ (DE) + out-continue-en/ (EN — guardrail verified).
  • humanization levers in use: voice marin, semantic VAD (eagerness: medium), near_field noise reduction, echo-cancelled mic constraints; captions via gpt-4o-mini-transcribe.
  • her name is Maya (client decision 2026-07-07: "Call her Maya"): in the greeting, the Role section and the honesty rule — asked "am I talking to a real person?" she answers as Maya, the hotel's digital assistant (verified DE: "Mein Name ist Maya — ich bin die digitale Empfangsdame … Sie sind nicht mit einem Menschen verbunden"); regression #8 locks name + disclosure. (fun fact: asked to name herself, gpt picked Anna/Mara — the client called Maya.)
  • opening line: "Hi, welcome to Park Hotel Konz — I'm Maya, how can I help you today? … Sie können auch gerne Deutsch sprechen." — spoken once, live, by the model as the call opens (opening the panel starts the call — client decision 2026-07-07; the earlier cached on-open WAV was retired the same day: it played outside the call, so the model didn't know it had greeted and re-greeted, and its speaker audio leaked past echo cancellation into the mic, garbling the guest's first words); then she follows the guest's language.
  • quality bar: a real receptionist — natural, low latency; final German judged by native speakers (hotel staff), not by spec sheets (production-persona samples: test_users/gpt-realtime/out-delivery/*.wav; earlier rig-persona set: out/de-*.wav).
  • GDPR status (owner item, §10): usable now under OpenAI's DPA + SCCs (standard EU practice); formal EU data residency is sales-gated per project — needs owner sign-off before real-guest production.
  • privacy rule (decided 2026-07-07): no call-transcript capture. guest transcripts stream browser↔OpenAI for live captions only and are never stored server-side; a transcript-based learning loop was considered and ruled out on privacy grounds — persona improvements come from the free text regression suite + paid listening samples instead.

5 the MCP tools (8 registered, all complete)

what MCP is, and why we use it: MCP (Model Context Protocol) is a standard way to give an AI hands — each ability is a typed function ("tool") the model can call; the tool returns data, code renders it, and the model may only narrate what came back. why: grounding (every fact/price must arrive through a tool → nothing is invented), auditability (every call + payload is recorded as toolTrace — §11 shows them verbatim), and reuse (the same 8 tools serve the HTTP loop, the realtime call, and any external MCP client via node mcp/server.js). booking inputs are a structured contract (mcp/lib/booking-requirements.js): tool schemas, the prompt's asking-rules and the tool-boundary rejection are all generated from one file — party size can never be assumed (a code rail also rejects booking calls whose party size the guest never expressed). all 8 endpoints ✅ complete:

search_rooms — live availability + prices for a stay (booking engine)
  • holds/does: nothing static — every call runs a live SiteMinder capture (30-min cache + warm sweep, §4 speed layer): per room → available?, units left, fitsParty, live total + per-night rates (feeding the price menu), photos/amenities merged from the scraped data; renders the room cards.
  • when the AI calls it: open-ended availability/booking asks ("what's free…?") — but only AFTER the guest stated dates + party size (schema requires them; the tool rejects calls without them).
  • example: guest: "what's available Jul 24–26 for 2 adults?" → search_rooms(check_in, check_out, adults:2, children:0) → returns Comfort Double Plus ✓ €240 total, others sold out → she recommends it, cards appear, price spoken via {{P1}} marker.
check_availability — one specific room open? at what rate
  • holds/does: same live capture, filtered to one room: available/units/rates + capacity note when the party exceeds the room ("NOT sold out — too small for this group"); returns photos + booking link and renders that room's card so "it's on your screen" is always true.
  • when: the guest names a room ("is the Basic Apartment free…?"); same guest-stated-inputs rule.
  • example: "Ist das Standard-Doppelzimmer am 24.–26. frei? (2 Erw.)" → sold out → she says so FIRST, then recommends the available alternative from the same capture.
get_room_details — one room's features (scraped, + live total if dates given)
  • holds: the scraped room sheet — photos (22 across 5 rooms), m², view, amenities list.
  • when: feature questions ("does the apartment have a kitchen?") — no dates needed.
  • example: "what's special about the Basic Apartment?" → get_room_details(room) → 30 m², kitchen, balcony + 6 photos → card renders, she describes only what the tool returned.
  • cards accumulate (audit fix 2026-07-07): five detail calls in one turn = five cards on screen — they used to overwrite each other, leaving only the last room while she said "all five are on your screen". a re-fetch now refreshes its card instead of duplicating, keeping any price already shown.
get_hotel_info — THE fact store (facts.json, topic-filtered)
  • holds: every hotel fact — check-in/out + key-box late arrival, reception hours, breakfast times + dietary, parking + e-bike charging, Wi-Fi, sauna (free for direct bookers), bar, pets (15 € fee — fee amounts feed the price menu), accessibility, children/extra beds, cancellation, direct-booking perks. the agent's prompt holds NONE of this — this tool is the only source, so every fact answer carries provenance.
  • also holds the general room-type list (live-call find 2026-07-07: no tool could answer "which rooms do you have?" without dates, so she honestly said she had no list): topic rooms → the 5 types with sqm/view/occupancy, deliberately no prices (prices need dates via search_rooms); edge-case #6 guards it. since 2026-07-08 the list also renders as photo cards (guest find: the answer was a wall of text, no images): a matched rooms topic puts one card per type on screen — photo, sqm/view/sleeps, feature pills, and a "for prices, just tell me your dates" label instead of a rate; both prompts tell her to speak a short overview and point to the cards. an untopiced all-facts answer never splashes cards.
  • when: any fact/schedule/policy/amenity question, every time (the suite fails fact answers without it).
  • example: "when is breakfast on the weekend?" → get_hotel_info(topic:"breakfast") → the breakfast block → "8:00 to 10:00" — never from memory.
  • screen rule (audit fix 2026-07-07): fact answers put NOTHING on the panel, so the prompt forbids "it's on your screen" after fact turns, and the harness flags any screen claim made before a UI payload ever appeared in the session.
create_payment_link — secure pre-filled checkout deep-link
  • holds/does: builds the official direct-book.com URL pre-filled with room + dates + occupancy — no reservation is created by us; card data NEVER touches the chat (she refuses spoken card numbers).
  • when: the guest says "I'll take it" after a grounded offer.
  • example: "I'll take the Comfort Plus without breakfast" → link returned → payment row renders with Pay ↗ → she says the link is on screen.
escalate_to_staff — hand the guest to humans (present-contact mode)
  • holds/does: the hotel's phone (+49 6501 6037987, landline, ~7:00–20:00) + email; returns the contact card (tel/mailto buttons) with the recommended channel by urgency; logs every escalation to leads/escalations.jsonl.
  • when: complaints/refunds, group deals, anything unresolvable, or any failed live lookup.
  • example: "letzte Woche war eine Katastrophe, ich will eine Erstattung" → contact card renders, she points to email for written matters.
check_promotions — direct-booking perks (scraped, real)
  • holds: the site's published direct-booking perks (e.g. free sauna) — no invented discount codes.
  • when: "any deals/offers?" — grounded perks or honestly nothing.
  • example: "any specials?" → the perks list → she names them, nothing more.
